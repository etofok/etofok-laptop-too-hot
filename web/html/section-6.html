The Silicon Lottery

All components come out of the factory slightly different. Sometimes noticeably different. Like up to 20-30% different. This is often referred to as the 'silicon lottery'. My particular CPU can be 'better' than yours. Or not. 'Better' in a sense of potential top speeds and/or voltage numbers required. If you're lucky you can increase the frequency quite a lot. Or seriously lower the voltage. Or both. Or not. Who knows. But to account for this randomness, manufacturers pre-set voltages high enough so even the worst components are stable out of the gate. All components should work with 100% stability on a factory new laptop. The fact that most components can run much cooler is a lesser concern, than the fact that a customer may receive a factory-new laptop that isn't stable. Just imagine that. Thankfully for us that means two wonderful things: 1. Almost all components are pre-set to run hotter than they should. 2. Almost all components are pre-set to run at a lower speed than they could. That's why things like overclocking and undervolting are possible to begin with. We will discuss both. Starting with speed.
