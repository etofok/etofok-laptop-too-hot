Thermal paste - replacement walkthrough

This is a super valuable skill. Once you learn this power - you'll be able to do it for other people, which means you can make decent money as a side hustle or even a job. And this service is not cheap: call your local repair shop and ask how much they charge for this - you'll be surprised. Remember me when you buy your first lambo. - Prepare your workplace, keep in mind all the tips above. - Respect your workstation and laptop, don't be an animal. - Make absolutely sure the laptop is 100% off, not in sleep, not in hibernate. - Carefully place your laptop upside down on the cleaned up table. - Unscrew all the screws off the back plate. - Put the screws in some small box so you don't lose them. - Put the box at a distance so you don't spill it. - Grab a tool to open the case. Start from either side. I usually start where the headphone jack is. Crack the sides, then the front, then the back. - Somehow, gradually but surely it'll pop off. - Don't put things on wool or carpets. - Locate the battery - the big black box. You can't miss it - it's huge. - Disconnect the battery. There's exactly one cable that connects it to the motherboard. The cable has red-black mini-cables. The connector is big, you can't miss it. If you skip this step there's a good chance (not 100%, but very high) you'll short circuit something. If the laptop is in sleep (not in shutdown) the chance is 100%. - After you have disconnected the battery cable - press the power button a couple of times. To be dead sure, you can hold it for 10 seconds. This clears whatever the electric charge might still be present in the laptop. - Clean out the dust: canned air is ideal. - Do not vacuum clean it. - Take a photo in case you forget how to put it back together. - Undo the cables connecting the cooling system to the board. - Unscrew the cooling system, and slowly remove it off the board. Use both hands. - If thermal paste is dry it'll feel like the cooling system is glued to the components. I promise it's never glued. Wiggle it. - Use cotton pads to remove the old thermal paste. - Don't use water. You don't need to use any liquids. - Apply your fresh thermal paste to the CPU and GPU in a 50% length 'X' shape in the middle. - Do not spill TP on the motherboard. Don't leave it there if you did. Use cotton sticks / q-tips to remove it if needed. - Place the cooling system back. Be wary of the cables. - Screw the cooling system back in, reconnect the cables, refer to your photo if needed. - Connect the battery dead last. - Put the back plate on, but don't screw it yet. - Turn the laptop on - verify it boots. - All should be great unless you forgot to connect something. - Then you can screw the back plate. Done. Next time is going to be much faster.
