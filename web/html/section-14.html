Undervolting - intro

Now you've learned a plenty about managing CPU frequency. But that's just one side of this coin. In fact, we can lower our temperatures without sacrificing performance. By lowering the voltage. At least to a point. And to find that point - is to successfully undervolt. Think of a spectrum from 'too little voltage' to 'too much voltage'. It's the same spectrum as going from 'unstable and can't work' to 'fried and destoyed'. It's always a balance between stability, temperature and performance. And as mentioned earlier, a laptop in its factory configuration is always too hot. It has to be a little too hot. And with time, use and wear - it's only getting hotter. And that's a problem. We are going to tinker with voltage(-s). And we need to find that delicate spot of 'barely, but exactly enough'.
