Overclocking and Underclocking

To increase performance output of a component is to overclock it. For example, bumping up a component's operating frequency from 3.0 GHz to 3.5 GHz. Overclocking is a very common play for desktops. Why desktops? Because unlike laptops, these beasts can be fitted with some serious cooling - from massive fans to water. And that helps with heat. Desktops are also less crammed in terms of physical space. They are simply bigger and more spacious than laptops. Components are reasonably spaced apart from each other. All this allows for more airflow in-between components, which help with the cooling. There's also another one - if you manage burn your desktop CPU/GPU - you can replace it. It's expensive, but laptop CPU/GPU are literally soldered to the motherboard. So it's not as plug-n-play. To decrease performance output of a component is to underclock it. For example, nudging a component's operating frequency down from 3.5 GHz to 3.0 GHz. People also use "downclocking", but I don't. But why would anyone want to decrease performance?
