CPU - Why lower our performance?

I wanted to give you a quick-and-dirty example how frequency and voltage directly affect temperature. And that trick is a fantastic way to do it. Because you can turn it on and off and see the effect immediately. But really, why would you ever want to do that? To lose ~30% performance? Well, there are a couple of reasons. First, sometimes you just don't need that Turbo 4.1 GHz speed. 2.4 GHz might be good enough for the task. Second, it's not enjoyable to play on a heating pan. Not only it's physically uncomfortable - hot and loud, (especially if you're around other people) it's also a constant reminder that the laptop is being fried. Third, 95°C degrees is the heat ceiling for most laptops. When your laptop gets there you lose performance! The in-built safety mechanism tanks its own frequencies - hard - effectively underclocking itself on demand. This mechanism is called PROCHOT (literally 'processor hot'). All CPUs have it. That temp limit is usually 95°C. And you better hope it does underclock your CPU at 95°C! You don't want your components to heat up past the point of no return. And 95°C is very close. 90°C degrees is already very hot, let alone 95°C. Let alone 95°C that is sustained for a long time.
